Microbit Controller Help

Setup guide and wiring instructions for the BLE Robot Controller.

1. Overview

This web application acts as a Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) remote control for a BBC Microbit robot. It allows you to drive the robot using keyboard or touch controls and manipulate up to 4 servo motors connected to the Microbit's expansion pins.

2. Wiring the Microbit

To use the Manipulator Arm functions, you must wire your servo motors to specific pins on the Microbit. Ensure you are using an expansion board (like the LOFI Robot board or a standard breakout) that can power the servos, as the Microbit alone cannot power 4 motors.

Signal Pin Map

  • Servo 1: Connect Signal wire to PIN 13
  • Servo 2: Connect Signal wire to PIN 14
  • Servo 3: Connect Signal wire to PIN 15
  • Servo 4: Connect Signal wire to PIN 16

Power Connections

  • Red Wire (VCC): Connect to external 5V-6V power source (Battery pack).
  • Brown/Black Wire (GND): Connect to Ground (GND). Important: Share ground with Microbit.

3. Controls

Driving

  • W or ▲ : Drive Forward
  • S or ▼ : Drive Backward
  • A or ◀ : Turn Left
  • D or ▶ : Turn Right
  • SPACE : Honk Horn / Beep

Manipulator Arms (Servos)

  • U / 7 : Open/Close Pin 13
  • I / 8 : Open/Close Pin 14
  • O / 9 : Open/Close Pin 15
  • P / 0 : Open/Close Pin 16
